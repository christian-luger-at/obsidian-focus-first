# Prioritization methods

Focus First doesn't invent a productivity system — it makes a few time-tested ones effortless to run on your own tasks. This section explains **what each method is for** and **when to reach for it**, so the plugin's features make sense rather than feeling arbitrary.

## The core problem

You have more to do than time to do it. Two failure modes follow:

- **Urgency hijacks importance** — loud, deadline-driven tasks crowd out the quiet, important ones that actually move your life or work forward.
- **Effort gets spent unevenly** — you sink hours into low-payoff work and never notice the five-minute tasks that would have made a real difference.

Every method below is a lens that fights one of those. They're complementary, not competing.

## The methods at a glance

| Method | The question it answers | Best when… |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **[Eisenhower matrix](/methods/eisenhower)** | *Is this urgent, important, both, or neither?* | Your list is a mix of deadlines and long-term work and you keep firefighting. |
| **[Value / Effort matrix](/methods/value-effort)** | *What's the best return for my energy right now?* | You're choosing what to build/do next and want quick wins without falling into time sinks. |
| **[Daily-planning methods](/methods/daily-planning)** (Eat the Frog, Ivy Lee, MITs) | *Of everything, what do I actually do today — and in what order?* | You have a plan but still start the day unsure where to put your hands. |

A simple way to combine them: use a **matrix** (Eisenhower or Value/Effort) to decide *what deserves attention*, then a **daily-planning method** to turn that into *today's short, ordered list*.

## How this maps to Focus First

- The 2×2 view is the **matrix** — [switch its axes](/guide/matrices) between Eisenhower and Value/Effort from the header.
- The **[Focus section](/guide/focus-size-filters#focus-tasks-today-plan)** above the matrix is your **daily plan** — an ordered, numbered shortlist you build by tagging tasks `#focus`.
