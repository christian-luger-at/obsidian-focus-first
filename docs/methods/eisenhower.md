# The Eisenhower matrix

> "What is important is seldom urgent, and what is urgent is seldom important."
> — attributed to Dwight D. Eisenhower

## The idea

Most people sort their day by **urgency** — whatever is loudest and closest to a deadline gets done. The problem is that urgency and **importance** are different things, and the urgent-but-unimportant stuff quietly steals the time you meant to spend on what actually matters.

The Eisenhower matrix separates the two into a 2×2 grid so you can *see* the difference and act on it:

|               | Urgent                        | Not urgent                        |
| ------------- | ----------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Important** | **Do** — do it now            | **Schedule** — plan a time for it |
| **Not important** | **Delegate** — hand it off | **Eliminate** — drop it           |

## What each quadrant is really about

- **Do (urgent + important)** — genuine crises and real deadlines. Handle them now, but a life lived only here is reactive and exhausting. If everything is landing here, your planning is happening too late.
- **Schedule (important, not urgent)** — planning, deep work, relationships, prevention, learning. **This is the quadrant that pays off**, and the one urgency steals from. The whole point of the matrix is to protect time for it *before* these tasks become urgent.
- **Delegate (urgent, not important)** — interruptions, many "quick questions", some meetings. Urgent to someone, but not important to *your* goals. Hand off, automate, or batch them.
- **Eliminate (neither)** — busywork and distraction. Notice it and let it go without guilt.

## How to use it

1. Be honest about **importance** — importance is about your goals, not about who is asking.
2. Aim to spend more time in **Schedule** and less firefighting in **Do**.
3. Ruthlessly shrink **Delegate** and **Eliminate**.

## In Focus First

Focus First places every task into these quadrants **automatically**: a task is *urgent* if its due date is within your urgency threshold, and *important* if its priority is in your "important" list. See **[How tasks are classified](/guide/classification)** for the exact rules, and pin a task by hand with a quadrant tag or by dragging it.

When you want to think in impact rather than deadlines instead, switch the view to the **[Value / Effort matrix](/methods/value-effort)**.
